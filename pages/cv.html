Research and Projects

Visual Reconstruction with EEG

January 2025 – Present
Stevens Institute of Technology
GitHub: github.com/IsaacVanB/visual-reconstruction-eeg

  • Built a public Python/PyTorch machine learning pipeline for visual reconstruction from EEG recordings, including data loading, preprocessing, model training, evaluation, and visualization.
  • Trained neural-network models to classify visual stimuli and predict compressed image-feature representations from noisy EEG time-series data.
  • Applied PCA, normalization, and feature-standardization workflows to reduce high-dimensional image embeddings into trainable prediction targets.
  • Integrated checkpointing, metric reporting, reconstruction grids, classifier predictions, and confusion-matrix generation into reusable experiment scripts.
  • Combined predicted low-level image features and class-label information with Stable Diffusion to generate reconstructed images from EEG model outputs.
  • Organized the codebase with modular source files, configuration files, Git/GitHub version control, and reproducible experiment outputs.
